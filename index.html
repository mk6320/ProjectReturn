Who We Are

Lived Experience, Not Theory

We are people who understand maladaptive daydreaming from the inside — not as a theory, but as a lived experience.

Our Perspective

We don’t see maladaptive daydreaming as a defect. It’s a learned coping habit — a way the mind escaped when reality felt overwhelming. We don't believe in fighting the mind or forcing positivity. Instead, we focus on building a new relationship with attention.

Why We Built This

Most tools are too abstract or require deep emotional work. We built this app to be practical. Something you can use at work, during routine tasks, or in social situations — in the exact moments MD usually starts.

What We Believe

Progress happens through repetition, not insight.

Noticing and returning is enough.

Real life doesn’t need to be exciting to be real.

You don’t need to become someone else to stop.
